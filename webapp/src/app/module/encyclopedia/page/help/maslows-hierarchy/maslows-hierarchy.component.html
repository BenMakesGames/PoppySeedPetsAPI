Poppyopedia » Glossary » Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

One of the driving forces of your pets' behaviors is a model of Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs (a real theory from psychology).

Pets have four needs:

  1. Food
  2. Safety
  3. Love
  4. Esteem

When a lower-level need ("food" being the lowest) is not met, the pet becomes less concerned with higher-level needs.

For example, a pet which feels unsafe will be less concerned with its social/love needs, or its self-esteem, however will still worry about eating.

Mechanically, whenever an action causes a pet to gain or lose a need, this gain or loss is reduced depending on how many lower-level needs are not met.

Finally, Maslow theorized that only when a human's needs are all met will that human attempt to improve itself. Similarly, your pets will gain fewer experience points when their needs are not met; the more unmet needs, the fewer experience points a pet will gain.

Taking Care of Pets

Petting and praising your pets are both good ways to address their safety, love, and esteem needs!

Pets will go out hunting and gathering when hungry, but hand-feeding a pet also increases a pet's Affection for you, and makes it feel safer!
